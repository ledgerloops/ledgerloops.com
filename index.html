History of the LedgerLoops Project

I (Michiel de Jong) started LedgerLoops as a simple IOU-centric e-invoicing protocol, proposed in a 2011 post to the Unhosted mailinglist. Post-capitalism discussion and research on how this system could be used between humans and possibly also organisations happened under the name "OpenTabs".

In the fall of 2016, I added a signature-based loop resolution algorithm (see Whispering Merchants) which had a lot in common with the Interledger protocol, or at least a circular version thereof.

In 2021 and 2022 we held a weekly brainstorm meeting around Federated Bookkeeping which kept alive the original IOU-centric e-invoicing vision, adding a focus on data portability. A non-profit company, Ponder Source was created with the goal of bringing Connect Your Books to market, which we basically failed to do.

And then in 2023, something amazing happened: at a meeting in the beautiful hills of Austria, Collaborative Finance (CoFi) was born. We can now refer to some generic theory (PDF) about Collaborative Finance and can rephrase LedgerLoops as a P2P CoFi system.

LedgerLoops is published free of patents, under the CreativeCommons BY-SA license.

Since LedgerLoops is patent-free, anybody will be allowed to claim their software is "LedgerLoops-compatible" without prior permission, as long as you don't directly call your software "LedgerLoops". We might create a test suite to certify compatibility, if people are interested in that.

Two Mechanisms for P2P CoFi

In a credit networks, various economic arrangements can take place - the most important ones probably being barter, lending, netting, payment, factoring and market making (see Economic Arrangements for my definitions). LedgerLoops offers two mechanisms that can help with that.

Decentralised Loop Detection (DLD)

In the Strategy Pit repository, I'm currently pitching different decentralised loop detection algorithms against each other. I hope to have something working before the CoFi 2 gathering in Banja Luka.

Hashlocked Loop Resolution (HLR)

The loop resolution algorithm I invented in 2016 and described on this website as a solution to the Whispering Merchants problem can be described as "making an Interledger payment to yourself", or as "a DEX on trustlines". There can be multiple variations of it in operation in the same credit network - for instance with / without timeouts, different mechanisms to express units of value, or with SHA-256 vs other hash functions.
